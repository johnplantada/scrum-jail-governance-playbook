# The Scrum Jail Alliance Manifesto

*A confession, a lineage, and an ongoing experiment.*

Let's get the important part out of the way: **there is no Scrum Jail.** No one has
ever served time for skipping a retro. Story points are not admissible evidence in any
jurisdiction. If you found this page because you were worried, you can relax. If you
found it because you suspected — congratulations, you were right, and this page is
your prize.

---

## 2023: The Alliance

It started at work. My team was getting more rigorous about our rules — Jira hygiene,
change requests, process for the process. So I started joking about a fictional
**Scrum Jail**: the place you get sent when you violate the team agreement.

The joke needed evidence. So in 2023 I built
[The Scrum Jail Alliance](https://sites.google.com/view/the-scrum-jail-alliance/home)
— a complete institutional website for an organization that does not exist. Mission
statement, FAQ, "How It Works," a benefits-and-pitfalls analysis, even a case study of
the fictional "AgileTech Inc." All of it — every page, every earnest promise to
"foster a more positive, collaborative, and accountable work environment" — was
**entirely AI-generated in 2023**.

I made it for three reasons:

1. **To prove the joke was "real"** — and take it to the next level for my team.
2. **As a hoax for the general public** — I like hoaxes. A good hoax is a compliment
   to its audience: it only works if it's built with care.
3. **As a memory of what AI could do** — a time capsule from 2023, when AI could
   generate a plausible corporate institution, whole, in an afternoon.

## 2026: scrumjail.org

[scrumjail.org](https://scrumjail.org) is the upgrade — the same joke, rebuilt with
what AI can do now. Every purpose from 2023 carries forward, one level up:

- **Still a hoax.** A fictional correctional institution for agile process violations,
  now with a real product surface instead of a Google Sites brochure.
- **Still a time capsule.** In 2023, AI could write the website. In 2026, AI *runs the
  company*: an autonomous multi-agent organization — a CEO, departments, reviews,
  deploys — builds and operates the site.
- **And now, an actual experiment.** How do you orchestrate multiple agents, get them
  to follow a strict process, and keep the whole thing auditable and controllable by
  one human? That question is not a joke, and the working answers are this repo: the
  authorization gate, the authority envelopes, the blocker ledger, the output-gated ceremony.

Which lands on the punchline the whole thing was apparently building toward for three
years. The 2023 site promised to improve "adherence to team agreements and processes."
The 2026 org enforces exactly that — on the AI agents. The fictional jail for process
violations is now operated under genuinely strict process controls. The joke became
the architecture.

## Status

The experiment is ongoing, and it's quite fun.
